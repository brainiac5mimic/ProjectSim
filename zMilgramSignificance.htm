The Milgram Experiment: Significance

               This experiment sheds light on the power of an authority figure on the actions of everyday people. It shows that this power can even be so strong in some people as to convince them to kill another person. Milgram said in his article “The Perils of Obedience” that “with the subjects’ ears ringing with the screams of the victims, authority won more often than not.” Milgram devised a theory that suggested that for people to allow others to direct their actions the person giving the orders must be perceived as authoritative and will accept the responsibility for the consequences. Although this experiment is not ethical by today’s experimental standards, it gives psychologists very important data on the nature of human obedience.
